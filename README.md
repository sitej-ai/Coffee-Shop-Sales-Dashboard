# Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard (Excel)

## Project Overview
This project presents an interactive Excel dashboard analyzing coffee shop sales across three locations: Astoria, Hell’s Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan. The objective is to uncover actionable insights related to revenue trends, customer behavior, and product performance to support business decision-making.

---

## Business Problem
The business needs clarity on:
- Peak sales periods (time & day)
- Product performance across categories
- Location-wise differences in customer behavior
- Opportunities to improve underperforming periods

---

## Tools & Skills Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts  
- Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)  
- Data Visualization  
- Business Analysis  

---

## Dashboard Features
- Location-based filtering (Astoria, Hell’s Kitchen, Lower Manhattan)
- Monthly revenue trend analysis
- Transactions by day of the week
- Transactions by hour of the day
- Product category performance
- Top 15 products by revenue and transactions

## Key Insights & Strategic Recommendations

### Location Performance
- Revenue and transactions are evenly distributed across Astoria, Hell’s Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan
- Astoria slightly leads, but differences are marginal → operations are consistent across locations

**Strategy:**
- Introduce location-specific offerings to create differentiation and boost competitive advantage

---

### Revenue Trends
- Steady revenue growth from January to June
- Noticeable dip in February across all locations
- Peak revenue observed in June (~$54K–$57K)

**Strategy:**
- Launch targeted promotions or seasonal campaigns in February to reduce revenue dips

---

### Day-of-Week Patterns
- Lower Manhattan peaks on Monday → suggests commuter-driven demand
- Astoria performs best on weekdays (Mon/Thu)
- Hell’s Kitchen shows mixed peaks across the week
- Weekends underperform across all locations

**Strategy:**
- Introduce weekend-specific offers (combos, discounts) to improve low footfall
- Align staffing with weekday demand patterns

---

### Hour-of-Day Patterns
- Strong peak between 8 AM – 10 AM across locations
- Lower Manhattan shows sharp morning spikes (commuter trend)
- Hell’s Kitchen has more evenly distributed traffic

**Strategy:**
- Optimize staffing during morning peak hours to improve service efficiency
- Introduce quick-service or pre-order options for high-traffic periods

---

### Product Performance
- Coffee dominates all locations
- Tea is consistently the second-highest contributor
- Bakery items show strong upselling potential

**Strategy:**
- Bundle beverages with bakery items to increase average transaction value
- Promote high-performing categories through menu placement

---

### Top Products
- Barista Espresso is the top revenue-generating product
- Brewed Chai Tea and Hot Chocolate follow closely
- Sugar-free syrup is the lowest-performing product

**Strategy:**
- Focus marketing on top-performing products
- Re-evaluate or reposition low-performing items (e.g., sugar-free syrup)

---

## Limitations & Future Scope
- Analysis is limited to revenue; cost and profit data are not available  
- Customer-level data is not included, restricting deeper behavioral insights  
- Future work: Incorporate profit metrics and expand the dataset for more detailed analysis  

---

## Conclusion
This project demonstrates how Excel can be used to transform raw sales data into actionable business insights. The dashboard highlights key patterns in customer behavior, product performance, and revenue trends, enabling data-driven decision-making for business improvement.
